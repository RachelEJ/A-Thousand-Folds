Star Box

Being a traditional model, the star box is great for teaching someone origami when they get to the Intermediate level. It is recommended to practice the Star Box before trying the Iris/Lily due to some of the steps being carried over.

  • Step 1

    The paper here is 15cm x 15cm, single-sided origami paper. It is currently folded into the Square Base with the open end pointing away from you.

  • Step 2

    Fold the top left edge to meet the vertical center line and crease.

  • Step 3

    Repeat with the right edge.

  • Step 4

    Take the left flap, squash fold, then crease.

  • Step 5

    Repeat on the right side.

  • Step 6

    Turn the model over.

  • Step 7

    Fold the top edges to the vertical center line.

  • Step 8

    Squash fold and crease.

  • Step 9

    Fold the topmost right flap over to the left side. Fold the bottommost left flap over to the right side. You should see a smooth side as pictured.

  • Step 10

    Fold the top left edge down to meet the vertical center line.

  • Step 11

    Repeat on the right side.

  • Step 12

    Turn the model over.

  • Step 13

    Repeat Steps 10 and 11.

  • Step 14

    Fold the bottom triangle up and crease. Make sure to keep the vertical center line continuous. Unfold.

  • Step 15

    Fold the top corner down and crease. Maintain the vertical center line.

  • Step 16

    Repeat on the other three sides.

  • Complete

    Open the flaps to stand at 90Â° while flattening the bottom simultaneously. You have completed the Star Box.
