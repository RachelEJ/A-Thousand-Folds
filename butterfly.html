Butterfly

The traditional butterfly is one of the many different ways to make a butterfly. While others may be more popular, this one is very pretty for its simplicty.

  • Step 1

    The paper here is 15cm x 15cm, single-sided origami paper. Any paper cut into the shape of a square should be usable.

  • Step 2

    Turn the paper over.

  • Step 3

    Fold the top down to meet the bottom edge. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 4

    Turn 90Â°. Fold the top down to meet the bottom. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 5

    Fold the top edge down to meet the horizontal center line. Crease then unfold..

  • Step 6

    Fold the bottom edge up to meet the horizontal center line. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 7

    Fold the right edge over to meet the vertical center line. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 8

    Fold the left edge over to meet the vertical center line. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 9

    Fold the top left corner down to meet the bottom right corner. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 10

    Fold the top right corner down to meet the bottom left corner. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 11

    Turn the paper over.

  • Step 12

    Fold the top right corner down to meet the center point. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 13

    Fold the top left corner down to meet the center point. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 14

    Fold the bottom left corner down to meet the center point. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 15

    Fold the bottom right corner down to meet the center point. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 16

    Turn the paper over.

  • Step 17

    Fold the left and right sides in towards the middle to meet the vertical center line. Only crease the lines in the middle.

  • Step 18

    Fold the bottom edge up over the left and right folded edges to meet the horizontal center line. The diagonal folds should also pull the edges of the paper towards the left and right sides as pictured.

  • Step 19

    Fold the top edge down over the left and right folded edges to meet the horizontal center line.

  • Step 20

    THe model should look as pictured. This is known by some as the "double boat base," but the Traditonal Butterfly is the only tutorial on this website that uses it.

  • Step 21

    Fold the bottom right and left corners down so that the top edges meet the vertical center line. Flatten the model.

  • Step 22

    Turn the model over.

  • Step 23

    Fold the top edge down to meet the bottom edge of the square.

  • Step 24

    Turn the model over.

  • Step 25

    This step includes measurements for those who prefer exact reference points. If you would rather fold the model on your own, you are welcome to. Mentally divide the bottom right edge of the triangle into 4 segments. Now draw an imaginary line from the start of the second segment to the top right corner of the square.

  • Step 26

    Valley fold along that line and crease.

  • Step 27

    Repeat on the left side.

  • Step 28

    Turn the model over.

  • Alternate View

    The next two steps are more optional measurements. Feel free to wing it. Divide the line between the vertical center line and the right edge of the square into 8 segments.

  • Step 30

    Imagine a line from the center of the bottom edge of the square to the start of the third segment.

  • Step 31

    Valley fold on that line. Crease and unfold.

  • Step 32

    Repeat on the other side.

  • Step 33

    Make sure the model is symmetrical.

  • Complete

    Pinch the middle of the model to make the butterfly's body protrude. You have completed the Butterfly.
