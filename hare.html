Dollar Bill Hare

The Dollar Bill Hare, designed by Barth Dunkan, is true to its name in that it is meant to be folded from a dollar bill. Because of this, a regular square sheet of paper cannot be used unless it is cut into the ratio of a dollar bill. The first few steps of this tutorial teach how to cut a square sheet of paper into a piece with a dollar bill's ratio. If you are using a dollar, skip to

Step 11

  • Step 1

    The paper here is 15cm x 15cm, single-sided origami paper. Any paper cut into the shape of a square should be usable.

  • Step 2

    Fold the top right corner down to the bottom left corner and crease.

  • Step 3

    Unfold.

  • Step 4

    Bring the top edge to meet the diagonal line, but DO NOT crease all the way. Only pinch the right corner.

  • Step 5

    Unfold.

  • Step 6

    Using the pinch on the right side as an axis, fold the top down and crease.

  • Step 7

    Turn the model over.

  • Step 8

    Fold the excess up and crease.

  • Step 9

    Unfold and turn the paper over.

  • Step 10

    Cut the paper on the horizontal lines. Discard the top strip and you have two pieces of paper with the same ratio as a dollar bill.

  • Step 11

    Start with one piece of paper with the ratio of a dollar bill.

  • Step 12

    Turn the paper over.

  • Step 13

    Fold the top edge down to meet the bottom and crease.

  • Step 14

    Unfold.

  • Step 15

    Fold the top and bottom edges in to meet the horizontal center line. Crease and unfold.

  • Step 16

    Rotate 90°.

  • Step 17

    Fold the top edge over to meet the right edge. Crease and unfold.

  • Step 18

    Fold the top edge over to meet the left edge and crease.

  • Step 19

    Unfold.

  • Step 20

    Imagine a line running between the bottom of the diagonal creases. Valley fold along that line and crease.

  • Step 21

    Unfold.

  • Step 22

    Rotate the paper 180°.

  • Step 23

    Repeat Steps 17 through 21.

  • Step 24

    Fold the top edge down to meet the square's bottom edge. Crease and unfold.

  • Step 25

    Fold the top edge down to meet the square's horizontal center line and crease.

  • Step 26

    Use the previous fold to half the second half of the square and crease.

  • Step 27

    Unfold. The square should have four quarters running horizontally.

  • Step 28

    Rotate the paper 180° and repeat Steps 24 through 27.

  • Step 29

    Fold the left and right sides of the bottom three segments up at 90° angles. Valley fold along the diagonals right above as well.

  • Step 30

    Fold the left and right sides of the bottom three segments to meet the vertical center line.

  • Step 31

    Flatten the model.

  • Step 32

    Mountain fold along the start of the new section and crease.

  • Step 33

    Fold the bottom edge up to the bottom edge of the main body and crease.

  • Step 34

    Slightly unfold Step 33 and valley fold along the diagonals of the small square while keeping the top edge in contact with the bottom of the main body. This step is similar to one in the Traditional Butterfly model.

  • Step 35

    Rotate the model 180°.

  • Step 36

    Repeat Steps 29 through 31.

  • Step 37

    Repeat Step 32.

  • Step 38

    Repeat Steps 33 and 34.

  • Step 39

    Fold the top two flaps upwards and crease.

  • Step 40

    Turn the model over.

  • Step 41

    Take the small triangles at the bottom of the model. Fold the outer corner in to the inner corner as pictured and crease.

  • Step 42

    Fold the tip of the small triangle out towards the side as pictured.

  • Step 43

    Turn the model over.

  • Step 44

    Fold the left edge of the left ear to meet the right edge. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 45

    Fold the right edge of the right ear to meet the left edge. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 46

    The model should look at pictured.

  • Step 47

    Mountain fold along the line from the top of the white rectangle to the start of the top region.

  • Step 48

    At the same time, mountain fold the vertical center line.

  • Step 49

    Collapse the sides over with a valley fold running from the top inner corner to the bottom outer corner.

  • Step 50

    Flatten and crease all edges.

  • Step 51

    On the front left leg, fold the flap forwards while using the bottom corner and the intersection with the head as reference points. Crease then unfold.

  • Step 52

    Open the flaps on the head and the leg.

  • Step 53

    Tuck the part from Step 51 under the head.

  • Step 54

    Flatten the model and crease all new edges.

  • Step 55

    Turn over and repeat Steps 51 through 54.

  • Step 56

    Mountain fold the excess inwards on both sides.

  • Step 57

    Turn the model over. Fold the corner to the left and crease.

  • Step 58

    Open the back and start to inside reverse fold the corner.

  • Step 59

    Pull a smaller corner out to form the tail. Crimp fold and crease.

  • Step 60

    Mountain fold the sides of the ears and press down towards the head to form that triangle.

  • Step 61

    Double rabbit ear fold and crease.

  • Step 62

    Valley fold along the vertical center line to make the ears point forward.

  • Step 63

    Fold over a corner on the top of the head. Crease and unfold.

  • Step 64

    Round off the previously folded corner.

  • Step 65

    Make small mountain folds at the corners of the nose to keep it in place. The model will no longer lie flat.

  • Step 66

    Fold under the excess.

  • Step 67

    Shape the legs.

  • Step 68

    Shape the body.

  • Complete

    You have completed the Dollar Bill Hare.
